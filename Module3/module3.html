Module 3: Social Media & Debate Skills

Goal: To explore the impact of social media and strengthen my speaking and argumentative skills in English.

Home / Module 3

My Favourite App

Reflection

This activity helped me describe my personal experience with Reddit in English. I practised using present simple and frequency expressions like “I use it twice a day.” It was useful to connect vocabulary with my daily routine and to express opinions clearly. One positive aspect was identifying both the features I enjoy and the negative side, such as the toxic environment.

Twitter/X Essay

Reflection

This activity helped me organise ideas into paragraphs and use linking expressions like “In the first place,” “However,” and “On balance.” I practised writing about advantages and disadvantages, focusing on clarity and coherence. It was useful to reflect on how social media can inform but also mislead, and to express a balanced opinion at the end.

Negative Aspects of Social Media

Reflection

This activity helped me express cause and effect using structures like “may contribute to” and “can cause.” I practised listing negative impacts of social media, such as anxiety, misinformation, and reduced real-life interaction. It was useful to connect grammar with real-world issues and to write in a more formal tone.

Debate Preparation

Reflection

This activity helped me prepare arguments and anticipate counterarguments. I practised using expressions like “I strongly believe” and “On the other hand.” It was useful to improve my speaking fluency and to learn how to respond respectfully during a debate. One positive aspect was receiving high scores in preparation and rebuttal.

My Debate Notes

Reflection

This activity helped me summarise key ideas and organise my thoughts before speaking. I practised writing short, clear arguments and using topic-specific vocabulary. It was useful to prepare mentally and to feel more confident during the debate. One positive aspect was learning how to structure a persuasive speech.

Speaking Evaluation: Debate Prep

Reflection

This activity helped me practise pronunciation, fluency, and discourse management. I received full marks, which shows that I was able to express myself clearly and confidently. It was useful to rehearse before the final debate and to get feedback on my performance. One positive aspect was knowing that my preparation paid off.

Speaking Evaluation: Final Debate

Reflection

This activity helped me apply everything I had practised: vocabulary, structure, tone, and confidence. I spoke clearly and responded to others with respect and logic. It was useful to experience a real debate and to reflect on how to improve. One positive aspect was feeling proud of my progress in spoken English.

Final Reflection

This module helped me combine writing, critical thinking, and speaking skills in English. I learned to analyse social media and express my ideas clearly in both written and oral formats. The activities were useful to connect English with real-life situations in Artigas and to prepare for future academic challenges.

Vocabulary Log

  • Misinformation: false or misleading information shared online.
  • Toxic environment: space where insults and harassment are common.
  • Addiction: excessive use of social media that affects daily life.
  • Rebuttal: response to counter an argument in a debate.
  • Discourse management: ability to organise and connect ideas when speaking.
