Module 4: Horror Story — Halloween Special

Goal: To explore global horror legends and create an original story inspired by real myths.

Reading Activity

Reflection

This activity helped me practise reading comprehension and extract key details from a narrative. I focused on identifying emotions, setting, and character reactions. It was useful to analyse how fear is built through description and pacing. One positive aspect was learning how to retell a ghost story using my own words.

Planning My Horror Story

Reflection

This activity helped me brainstorm ideas and organise my thoughts before writing. I practised using descriptive adjectives and visual elements to create a scary character. It was useful to connect imagination with structure. One positive aspect was inventing the Wendigo-inspired creature with symbolic features like empty eyes and deer horns.

Horror Story Draft

Reflection

This activity helped me develop narrative skills and write a short horror story in English. I practised using past tense, sequencing, and suspense techniques. It was useful to combine cultural references with psychological explanations. One positive aspect was exploring the Wendigo myth through both fiction and science.

Scientific & Psychological Theories

Reflection

This activity helped me connect storytelling with scientific reasoning. I practised summarising theories like cultural disorders and environmental causes of hallucinations. It was useful to reflect on how myths can be explained through psychology. One positive aspect was learning to balance imagination with logic.

Speaking Evaluation

Reflection

This activity helped me practise oral delivery and pronunciation. I focused on clarity, tone, and pacing while presenting my story. It was useful to receive feedback and understand how to improve my performance. One positive aspect was sharing my story with confidence and creativity.

Final Reflection

This module helped me explore horror storytelling from different angles: reading, planning, writing, and speaking. I learned how to build suspense, describe emotions, and connect myths with real-world explanations. It was useful to practise both creative and analytical skills in English.

End-of-Year Reflection

This year I worked on multiple modules that combined language learning with personal expression. I improved my writing, speaking, and critical thinking skills. Each activity helped me connect English with my life. I feel proud of my progress and ready for future challenges.

Vocabulary Log

  • Apparition: a ghostly figure appearing unexpectedly.
  • Psychosis: a mental disorder causing loss of contact with reality.
  • Hallucination: perception of something that is not present.
  • Folkloric archetype: a recurring symbolic figure in traditional stories.
  • Somatic delusion: false belief related to the body, often seen in extreme cases.
  • Atmospheric tension: emotional pressure created by setting and mood.
  • Cultural contagion: spread of beliefs or behaviours through social influence.
